F.R.I.E.N.D.S

Friends is, in my opinion, the best sitcom of all time. I absolutely adore it, and whenever I replay Friends, it always makes me cry.

Friends is one of my favorite shows since it truly perfected the sitcom format. It wasn’t as inventive as Seinfeld, but it mastered the sitcom structure in my opinion. It is, without a doubt, comfort food.

Let’s come to the parts that I enjoyed the most, which are, of course,

1. Characters and their Interaction
I genuinely adore all of the characters in this program (except Mark of course).
The casting is excellent, and I really like how the performers embody their roles. The actors all put their hearts and souls into their characters, and they’re all quite friendly to one another.The chemistry between the characters, in my opinion, is what distinguishes the characters. Each of the characters has a unique and endearing chemistry with one another. Ross and Rachel are a very legendary pair, despite their destructive relationship. Monica and Chandler have the show’s best relationship. I really enjoyed how Joey and Phoebe complement each other perfectly. Monica and Rachel are wonderful female pals. Joey and Chandler are the ideal brotherly duo.

While the characters are “overexaggerated” in the last season, they are still likable and interesting. They have a lot of depth to them as well. Chandler hides his insecurity and shameful past with sarcasm, Rachel has a thing for doctors because her father is one, Phoebe had a traumatic past but chose to have a positive outlook, Joey is the way he is because of his family, and Ross and Monica are super competitive because of their toxic family structure. Everyone has reached their full potential.

In addition, the supporting characters are notable. Gunther, Mike, Ursula, The Gellers, Chandler’s parents, Joey’s family, Richard, Janice, and others were among the guests.

I also loved how this program addressed toxic masculinity, such as Ross’ dissatisfaction with the male nanny and Ben’s playing with Barbie, as well as parenting, such as Phoebe’s surrogacy, Monica’s adoption, and Rachel’s single parenthood.

2. Consistency
Friends season 9 was, without a doubt, the worst.

However, I believe it is superior to Office Season 8. Monica and Chandler taking a fertility test, Monica singing, Phoebe meeting Mike, and Rachel’s other sister are among the cast members. But the point is that Friends is a constant in my life. Yes, there were some bumps in the road, but Monica and Chandler’s wedding, as well as Ross and Rachel’s kid, salvaged the series. But one thing I’d want to bring out is how consistent the series is. Like Game of Thrones or The Office, it doesn’t have any weak points for me. It’s still riding that high-quality wave.

I also enjoyed how this show coined new terminology that we are all familiar with. The terms “BFF” and “Friend Zone” are used interchangeably.
